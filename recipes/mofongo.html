Mofongo

Description

An afro-puertorrican dish with origins traced back to Africa's Fuf√∫.

At its most basic, mofongo is made of fried green plantains mashed with garlic and chicharrones (deep-fried pork skin),

served with a buttery-garlic or a peppery capsicum-tomato Creole sauce.

Ingredients

  • 3 Canola oil for frying
  • 3 Cloves garlic, or to taste
  • 3 Tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/8 Cup crushed fried pork skins
  • 2 Green plantains, peeled and sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat canola oil in deep fryer or saucepan to 350 degrees.
  2. Mash the garlic with the olive oil in a mortar and pestle.
  3. Combine garlic mixture with the pork rinds in a large bowl; set aside.
  4. Fry the plantain chunks until golden and crispy, but not brown, about 15 minutes.
  5. Transfer the fried plantains into the bowl with the garlic mixture. Toss to coat.
  6. Mash the coated plantains with the mortar and pestle until smooth.
  7. Season with salt.
  8. Roll the plantain mixture into two large balls or several small balls before serving.
